Welcome

Welcome to the official page for Proposition G! On April 8, 2025, Wright City R-II School District voters will decide on a no-tax-increase bond to fund a new performance gymnasium and other improvements at Wright City High School. Explore the details below and join us in supporting our students!

Prop G

Proposition G is an $11,000,000 general obligation bond to construct, improve, repair, renovate, furnish, and equip school facilities, including a new performance gymnasium at Wright City High School. Best of all, it won't raise your taxes—the debt service levy stays at $0.9995 per $100 of assessed valuation.

Ballot Language

Shall Wright City R-II School District of Warren County, Missouri issue its general obligation bonds in the amount of $11,000,000 for the purpose of constructing, improving, repairing, renovating, furnishing and equipping new and existing school facilities, including constructing a performance gymnasium at the High School?

If this question is approved, the District's debt service property tax levy is estimated to remain unchanged at $0.9995 per $100 dollars of assessed valuation of real and personal property.

Props Q & A

For more detailed questions and answers, visit the official Props Q & A page.

What is Prop G funding?

An $11M bond for a new performance gymnasium and other school facility upgrades.

Will my taxes go up?

No, the tax levy stays at $0.9995 per $100 of assessed value.
