Wright City Soccer Rules

General

  1. Equipment: Shin guards must be worn at all times. Jewelry is not to be worn at any time during play. All field players must wear the same color shirt (numbers are NOT required). Goalkeepers must wear a shirt of a different color from both teams.
  2. Touchline (Sideline) Restarts: Standard two-hand overhead throw-ins at the spot of the ball. A player may not directly throw a ball into the goal from its stationary restart position. The throw-in player may not be the first to touch the ball after the throw-in.
  3. Direct Restarts: A Corner Kick and Kickoff are Direct Kicks. The kick-off may go in any direction.
  4. Free Kick: A free kick is given at the spot of foul or handball. A free kick must not be directed towards the goal. A pass is required before scoring. The defense must be at least 5 yards away. A foul or handball inside the penalty area results in a free kick at the top of the penalty area.
  5. Goal Kick:  A goal kick is awarded when the ball crosses the goal line, last touched by an attacking player. The goal kick should be taken from anywhere within the goal area. Any player from the defending team can take the goal kick. In accordance with the Build Out rule, the opposing team must be behind the mid-field line until the ball is in play.
  6. Build Out: The defending team must be actively moving back behind the mid-field line on goalie possession or goal kick. The defending team may cross the line once the ball is thrown or kicked.
  7. Substitutions: Subs for either team may be made with the referees acknowledgement after any deadball. Deadball situations include when the ball goes out of play and after a goal is scored.
  8. Fouls: A foul will result in an indirect Free Kick. Players should be reminded about proper behavior.
  9. Duration: 2, 20 minute halves; running clock; 5-minute half-time
  10. Ties: Games can end in a tie.
  11. Offside Rules: There is no offside rule.
  12. Penalty Kick: There are no penalty kicks. See Free Kick.
  13. Have fun! These kids are here to play soccer and have fun.

Fair Play and Sportsmanship

  1. Playing time: Coaches are required to ensure equal and fair playing time among the players.
  2. Time Adjustments: Consider more frequent breaks if one team has fewer subs to compensate for fatigue and water breaks.
  3. Player Balancing: With mutual agreement from coaches, move players to the team with fewer players. This allows everyone more playing time and a better experience for the players.
  4. Good sportsmanship: After the game, players from both teams should line up and shake hands.

This is an educational league. Take time to correct the play so the kids can learn and improve their skills.

Expectations

  1. Conduct: Coaches are to lead by example and are therefore responsible for the conduct of the players and parents of their teams. The Referee and Staff will not tolerate unruly behavior and reserve the right to remove any Coach, Player, or Parent from the premises. This is an educational rec league.
  2. Coaching: All coaches, 8u and up need to stay on the sideline.
  3. Player’s sideline: For 8u and up, both teams will be on the same side of the field. Parents and spectators sit on the other side across from the players.
  4. Field of play: No coaches, players, or parents may stand by the goals.
